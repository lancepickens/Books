A Critical Compilation

Low-Energy Nuclear Reactions

What the peer-reviewed record actually says about nuclear anomalies in metal–hydrogen systems — the 1989 crisis, the strongest evidence, the modern reassessment, the theoretical gap, and where a breakthrough would plausibly come from. Something to play with in every chapter.

10 chapters · in browser · self-contained

  1. I The Yardstick: Fusion and the Coulomb Barrier Tunneling, the Gamow factor, and the canonical D+D numbers every later claim must be measured against.
  2. II 1989: Palladium, Heavy Water, and a Scientific Crisis What Fleischmann and Pons claimed, what the replications found, and what two DOE reviews concluded.
  3. III The Art of the Calorimeter How excess heat is measured, how measurements go wrong, and the strongest surviving datasets.
  4. IV The Claimed Nuclear Evidence Helium correlated with heat, tritium, transmutation, and energetic particles — claim by claim, critique by critique.
  5. V Loading the Lattice Palladium–deuterium as a materials problem: loading thresholds, electrochemistry, and why most cathodes fail.
  6. VI Electron Screening: The Accepted Anomaly Accelerator experiments show metals really do enhance fusion — by a real, measured, and stubbornly insufficient amount.
  7. VII The Reassessment: Google and NASA The Nature 2019 program that found no excess heat but better tools, and NASA's lattice-confinement fusion in Phys. Rev. C.
  8. VIII New Programs: Japan, Europe, ARPA-E Nano-composite heat claims from Tohoku and Clean Planet, the CleanHME project, and the 2023 ARPA-E portfolio.
  9. IX Theories and the Explanatory Gap Screening, phonons, Widom–Larsen — every proposed mechanism, how far short it falls, and the results that outrun all of them.
  10. X Where the Breakthroughs May Come From Informed speculation, clearly labeled: the materials, detection, and program designs most likely to settle the question.

Conventions used throughout: energies in $\mathrm{eV}$/$\mathrm{keV}$/$\mathrm{MeV}$; cross sections $\sigma$ in barns ($1\,\mathrm{b}=10^{-28}\,\mathrm{m^2}$); astrophysical factor $S(E)$ in $\mathrm{keV\cdot b}$ with $\sigma(E) = \dfrac{S(E)}{E}\,e^{-2\pi\eta}$; Sommerfeld parameter $\eta$; screening energy $\Ue$ in eV; Gamow energy $\EG$; loading as the atomic ratio $\DPd$ (D per Pd); excess power $\Pxs$ in watts. Nuclides are written $\nuc{4}{He}$; "D" is the deuterium atom, "d" the deuteron. Useful constants: $e^2/4\pi\epsilon_0 = 1.44\,\mathrm{eV\cdot nm}$; $\mathrm{D+D}$ Q-values $4.03\MeV$ (t+p), $3.27\MeV$ ($\nuc{3}{He}$+n), $23.85\MeV$ ($\nuc{4}{He}+\gamma$). Audience: a motivated polymath comfortable with calculus and introductory physics; every domain term is defined where it first appears. Chapters IX–X clearly separate established results, contested claims, and speculation. Appendix: how this book was researched.
