Chapter XIV

Black Holes and Strominger–Vafa

Black holes have entropy proportional to area. Strominger and Vafa, in 1996, built a specific black hole out of wrapped D-branes and counted its microscopic states. The numbers agreed exactly.

§1The puzzle of black-hole entropy

Hawking and Bekenstein, between 1972 and 1975, established that a black hole has an entropy proportional to the area of its event horizon:

$$ S_{\mathrm{BH}} \;=\; \frac{A}{4 G \hbar} \;=\; \frac{A}{4 \ell_P^2}. $$

Where

$A$
Surface area of the event horizon.
$\ell_P = \sqrt{\hbar G / c^3}$
Planck length, recovered (Chapter I) as the natural scale of quantum gravity. In units $\hbar = c = 1$, $\ell_P = \sqrt{G}$.

This is a serious surprise. Entropy in statistical mechanics counts microstates: $S = \ln \Omega$, where $\Omega$ is the number of distinguishable microscopic configurations consistent with the macroscopic state. For a black hole described purely by general relativity, the only macroscopic data are its mass, charge, and angular momentum (the "no-hair" theorem). What are the $e^{S_{\mathrm{BH}}}$ microstates the entropy is counting?

Solving this puzzle is one of the historic motivations for quantum gravity. From the 1970s onward, microstate counting was the gold-standard benchmark: any candidate theory of quantum gravity should derive $S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ from first principles, by enumerating its microscopic degrees of freedom.

§2The Strominger–Vafa setup

In January 1996, Andrew Strominger and Cumrun Vafa achieved this for the first time, for a particular five-dimensional black hole.

Take Type IIB string theory and compactify five dimensions: four into a $T^4$ and one into a small circle $S^1$ of radius $R$. The remaining non-compact spacetime is 5-dimensional. Inside this compactified theory, build a stack of branes:

  • $Q_1$ D1-branes wrapping the $S^1$,
  • $Q_5$ D5-branes wrapping the full $T^4 \times S^1$,
  • and momentum $N/R$ flowing along the $S^1$ direction (effectively $N$ units of KK momentum).

From the 5-d perspective this configuration looks like a single object — a point in 5-d spacetime carrying three conserved charges $(Q_1, Q_5, N)$. It is a BPS state: its mass saturates the bound set by its charges, so its properties are protected against the strong-to-weak-coupling extrapolation we are about to perform.

§3The macroscopic black hole

At strong string coupling $g_s$, the brane stack back-reacts on spacetime: it sources gravity, and the gravitational solution describes a 5-dimensional extremal black hole with horizon area

$$ A \;=\; 8 \pi^2 G_5 \sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N}, $$

(in suitable units), whose Bekenstein–Hawking entropy is

$$ S_{\mathrm{BH}} \;=\; \frac{A}{4 G_5} \;=\; 2 \pi \sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N}. $$

This is the macroscopic side of the calculation: a smooth GR computation, no string-theory specifics beyond the consistent embedding into supergravity.

§4The microscopic count

At weak string coupling $g_s$, the same configuration is not a black hole — the gravitational back-reaction is negligible and we can think of $Q_1$ D1-branes and $Q_5$ D5-branes sitting in flat space, with $N$ units of momentum running along them.

The bound state of $Q_1$ D1-branes inside $Q_5$ D5-branes, with momentum $N$, is the worldvolume theory of the D1-branes inside the D5-branes — a $(1+1)$-dim sigma model with target the symmetric product

$$ \mathrm{Sym}^{Q_1 Q_5}(T^4) \;=\; (T^4)^{Q_1 Q_5} / S_{Q_1 Q_5}, $$

at central charge $c = 6 Q_1 Q_5$. We need to count states of this 2-d CFT with momentum $N$.

The number of states at high momentum in a 2-d CFT is given by the Cardy formula,

$$ \Omega(N) \;\sim\; \exp\!\left( 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{c\, N}{6}} \right). $$

Substituting $c = 6 Q_1 Q_5$ gives

$$ \Omega(Q_1, Q_5, N) \;\sim\; \exp\!\left( 2\pi \sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N} \right), $$

and the microscopic entropy is

$$ \boxed{\; S_{\mathrm{micro}} \;=\; \ln \Omega \;=\; 2\pi \sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N}. \;} $$

§5The match

Compare:

$$ S_{\mathrm{BH}} \;=\; 2\pi\sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N} \;=\; S_{\mathrm{micro}}. $$

Exact, at leading order in large charges, with no free parameters. The macroscopic Bekenstein–Hawking formula and the microscopic count of brane bound-state configurations agree to the digit.

This was the first time the entropy of any black hole had been derived from a controlled microscopic theory of quantum gravity. The microstates the Bekenstein–Hawking formula was counting all along turned out to be the internal states of bound branes wrapping cycles of the compactification manifold.

§6What changed

The Strominger–Vafa calculation transformed the status of string theory:

  1. It established that string theory contains a complete microscopic description of (a class of) black holes, with quantum states one could count.
  2. It validated the BPS state-counting machinery: the count is protected by supersymmetry, so the same answer holds at any coupling — including the strong-coupling regime where the BH actually exists.
  3. It opened the door to more sophisticated calculations: subleading corrections, near-extremal black holes, and rotating cases were all worked out in the following years, with similar agreement.
  4. It motivated the more general holographic duality of Chapter XV: the same near-horizon physics that produces $S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ is the geometric reflection of a boundary CFT, and Maldacena's AdS/CFT proposal grew directly out of these brane-counting calculations.

Two important caveats: (a) only certain BPS or near-BPS black holes are tractable. Garden-variety astrophysical black holes (Schwarzschild, Kerr) are not BPS and the microstate count for them is not directly accessible by these methods. (b) Even for the BPS cases, the explicit construction of individual microstates — the "fuzzball" program of Mathur and others — remains an active research area, and the picture of which microstates the count is enumerating is still being refined.

§8What we carry forward

  1. Black holes have entropy $S_{\mathrm{BH}} = A/(4 G \hbar)$ from Bekenstein–Hawking, posing the question: what microstates is this counting?
  2. Strominger and Vafa (1996) constructed a 5-d extremal BPS black hole out of $Q_1$ D1-branes, $Q_5$ D5-branes, and momentum $N$, all wrapping compactification cycles.
  3. The microscopic count of bound states is given by the Cardy formula of a $(1+1)$-d CFT and equals $\exp(2\pi\sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N})$.
  4. This matches the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy $2\pi\sqrt{Q_1 Q_5 N}$ exactly, with no free parameters.
  5. This was the first first-principles derivation of black-hole entropy from quantum gravity, and the technical foundation for the AdS/CFT correspondence (Chapter XV).
