Chapter V

Symmetries of the Worldsheet

The Polyakov action has three independent worldsheet symmetries, just enough to make the metric $h_{ab}$ completely gauge — locally. What survives gauge-fixing is the infinite-dimensional conformal symmetry of two dimensions, the engine of everything that follows.

§1The three symmetries

The Polyakov action

$$ S_P[X, h] \;=\; -\frac{1}{4\pi\alpha'} \int_\Sigma \mathrm{d}^2\sigma\, \sqrt{-h}\, h^{ab}\, \partial_a X^\mu\, \partial_b X^\nu\, \eta_{\mu\nu} $$

is invariant under three groups of transformations.

Spacetime Poincaré invariance — a global symmetry. Under a Lorentz transformation $X^\mu \to \Lambda^\mu{}_\nu X^\nu$ and a translation $X^\mu \to X^\mu + a^\mu$, both $\partial_a X^\mu$ and $\eta_{\mu\nu}$ transform as tensors that contract back to invariants. This guarantees the target-space physics respects special relativity.

Worldsheet diffeomorphisms — reparametrizations $\sigma^a \to \sigma'^{a}(\sigma)$ of the worldsheet, under which

$$ X'^\mu(\sigma') = X^\mu(\sigma), \qquad h'_{ab}(\sigma') = \frac{\partial \sigma^c}{\partial \sigma'^a}\, \frac{\partial \sigma^d}{\partial \sigma'^b}\, h_{cd}(\sigma). $$

This is the 2-dim analogue of the 1-dim reparametrization invariance from Chapter III. It is generated by smooth vector fields $\xi^a(\sigma)$ on $\Sigma$, and it has two local parameters at each point — one for each coordinate.

Weyl rescaling — a local rescaling of the worldsheet metric while leaving $X^\mu$ alone:

$$ h_{ab}(\sigma) \;\to\; e^{2\omega(\sigma)}\, h_{ab}(\sigma), \qquad X^\mu(\sigma) \;\to\; X^\mu(\sigma). $$

This is invariance under "stretching" the worldsheet metric, where the stretch factor $e^{2\omega}$ depends on position. It changes lengths but preserves angles (the metric up to overall scale).

Why Weyl invariance works in 2D

Recall from Chapter IV §6 that $\sqrt{-h}\, h^{ab}$ is Weyl-invariant only in two dimensions: $\sqrt{-h} \to e^{2\omega}\sqrt{-h}$ and $h^{ab} \to e^{-2\omega} h^{ab}$, and the two factors cancel. In $d$ dimensions one gets $e^{(d-2)\omega}$, non-zero unless $d=2$. This is the technical reason the string action has an extra local symmetry the worldline action of an einbein does not.

Weyl is one local parameter. Together with the two parameters of diffeomorphism invariance, we have three local symmetries — exactly enough to fix the three independent components of the symmetric tensor $h_{ab}$.

§2Counting gauge freedom: $h_{ab}$ is pure gauge

The worldsheet metric $h_{ab}$ is a symmetric 2$\times$2 tensor — three independent functions of $(\tau, \sigma)$. We have three local symmetry parameters: two diffeomorphism vectors $\xi^a(\sigma)$ and one Weyl scalar $\omega(\sigma)$. The arithmetic works out:

$$ \text{components of } h_{ab}\;=\;3 \;\;=\;\; 2_{\text{Diff}} + 1_{\text{Weyl}}. $$

So $h_{ab}$ carries no physical information at all — every component can be fixed by a choice of gauge. The natural choice is the conformal (or flat) gauge,

$$ h_{ab}(\sigma) \;=\; \eta_{ab} \;=\; \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & +1 \end{pmatrix}. $$

This is always achievable locally — at any point of $\Sigma$ you can find coordinates and a Weyl factor that make $h_{ab}$ flat. Globally there may be topological obstructions (the moduli space of a torus, for instance, is non-trivial), but for our local analysis the worldsheet is just a flat 2-d Minkowski strip or cylinder.

§3The Polyakov action in conformal gauge

Substituting $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$ into $S_P$ collapses it dramatically:

$$ S_P^{\text{flat}}[X] \;=\; -\frac{1}{4\pi\alpha'} \int \mathrm{d}^2\sigma\, \eta^{ab}\, \partial_a X^\mu\, \partial_b X_\mu \;=\; \frac{1}{4\pi\alpha'} \int \mathrm{d}^2\sigma\, \big( (\partial_\tau X)^2 - (\partial_\sigma X)^2 \big). $$

This is $D$ free 2-d massless scalar fields living on the worldsheet. The whole machinery of 2-d quantum field theory is now available — propagators, normal-ordering, operator product expansions, the works. We will exploit this in Chapter VII.

The equations of motion are the free wave equation,

$$ \big( \partial_\tau^2 - \partial_\sigma^2 \big) X^\mu \;=\; 0, $$

solved by arbitrary left-movers $X_L^\mu(\tau + \sigma)$ and right-movers $X_R^\mu(\tau - \sigma)$.

§4The constraint we cannot forget

Gauge-fixing $h_{ab}$ is a choice. The equation of motion that produced $h_{ab}$ in the original Polyakov action — vary $\partial S_P/\partial h^{ab} = 0$ — still must hold, as a constraint on the surviving fields. That equation is

$$ T_{ab} \;\equiv\; \partial_a X^\mu\, \partial_b X_\mu - \tfrac{1}{2}\, h_{ab}\, h^{cd}\, \partial_c X^\mu\, \partial_d X_\mu \;=\; 0, $$

the worldsheet stress-energy tensor. After setting $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$ the constraint becomes

$$ T_{\tau\tau} \;=\; T_{\sigma\sigma} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\big( (\partial_\tau X)^2 + (\partial_\sigma X)^2 \big), \qquad T_{\tau\sigma} \;=\; \partial_\tau X \cdot \partial_\sigma X, $$

and both independent components must vanish:

$$ \big(\partial_\tau X\big)^2 + \big(\partial_\sigma X\big)^2 \;=\; 0, \qquad \partial_\tau X \cdot \partial_\sigma X \;=\; 0. $$

These are the classical Virasoro constraints. They are the worldsheet analogue of the point-particle mass-shell condition $p^2 + m^2 = 0$ (Chapter III §4): both are the algebraic content of having gauge-fixed a reparametrization symmetry. The string version is much richer because there are two coordinates to fix.

§5Light-cone coordinates

It is now elegant to switch to worldsheet light-cone coordinates,

$$ \sigma^\pm \;=\; \tau \pm \sigma, $$

with corresponding derivatives $\partial_\pm = \tfrac{1}{2}(\partial_\tau \pm \partial_\sigma)$. The wave equation factorizes,

$$ \partial_+ \partial_- X^\mu \;=\; 0, $$

making explicit the decomposition $X^\mu(\tau, \sigma) = X_L^\mu(\sigma^+) + X_R^\mu(\sigma^-)$. The stress tensor splits similarly: $T_{++}$ depends only on $\sigma^+$, $T_{--}$ only on $\sigma^-$, and the trace component $T_{+-}$ vanishes identically (this is the Weyl-invariance statement — tracelessness of the stress tensor). The Virasoro constraints become

$$ T_{++} \;=\; \partial_+ X^\mu\, \partial_+ X_\mu \;=\; 0, \qquad T_{--} \;=\; \partial_- X^\mu\, \partial_- X_\mu \;=\; 0. $$

Left- and right-moving sectors of the string each contribute an independent constraint. The string really is two non-interacting collections of free waves, tied together only by these constraints and (for the closed string) by level-matching, which we will meet in Chapter VII.

§6The residual symmetry: conformal transformations

Having fixed $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$, we have used up most of our gauge freedom — but not all of it. A combined diffeomorphism + Weyl rescaling that leaves $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$ unchanged is still a symmetry. What is the residual group?

Suppose we apply a diffeomorphism with $h_{ab} \to (\partial \sigma / \partial \sigma')_a^{\;c} (\partial \sigma / \partial \sigma')_b^{\;d}\, h_{cd}$, which generically takes $\eta_{ab}$ to some other matrix. We can recover $\eta_{ab}$ by composing with a Weyl transformation, but only if the diff produced a metric proportional to $\eta_{ab}$ — i.e., conformal to it. In light-cone coordinates this requirement is sharply simple:

$$ \sigma^+ \;\to\; \tilde\sigma^+(\sigma^+), \qquad \sigma^- \;\to\; \tilde\sigma^-(\sigma^-). $$

Any pair of (orientation-preserving) functions, one of $\sigma^+$ alone and one of $\sigma^-$ alone, is a residual symmetry. These are the conformal transformations of the 2-d worldsheet.

The 2D conformal miracle

In dimensions $d \geq 3$, the conformal group is finite-dimensional — for Minkowski signature it has $(d+1)(d+2)/2$ generators. In two dimensions, the conformal group becomes infinite-dimensional: arbitrary holomorphic functions on each light-cone direction. This is why 2-d conformal field theory has so much structure — Virasoro algebra, primary fields, the Belavin–Polyakov–Zamolodchikov bootstrap — all powered by this single fact. The string borrows it wholesale.

The infinitesimal version: vector fields $\xi^+(\sigma^+)\partial_+$ and $\xi^-(\sigma^-)\partial_-$. Their algebra, after quantization, is the Virasoro algebra (Chapter VII) — and the central extension of that algebra encodes the conformal anomaly that fixes the critical dimension (Chapter VIII).

§8What we carry forward

  1. $S_P$ has three local worldsheet symmetries: two diffeomorphism + one Weyl. They match the three components of $h_{ab}$ exactly; $h_{ab}$ is therefore pure gauge.
  2. The natural gauge choice is conformal gauge, $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$, in which the action reduces to $D$ free 2-d scalar fields obeying the wave equation $\partial_+ \partial_- X^\mu = 0$.
  3. The equation $T_{ab} = 0$ — the EOM for $h_{ab}$ in the un-gauge-fixed theory — survives as a constraint, the Virasoro constraints $T_{\pm\pm} = 0$.
  4. The residual gauge symmetry after fixing $h_{ab} = \eta_{ab}$ is the 2-d conformal group: independent holomorphic reparametrizations of $\sigma^+$ and $\sigma^-$. Infinite-dimensional. Unique to two dimensions.
  5. Quantizing this residual symmetry will produce the Virasoro algebra, with a central charge whose vanishing fixes $D$ (Chapter VIII).

With $h$ gauged away and the constraints articulated, the next chapter solves the free wave equation explicitly and reads off the spectrum of classical string vibrations.
