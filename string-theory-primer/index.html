A Primer

String Theory

A geometric climb from the action principle to holography — every equation explained, every symbol introduced, every chapter with something to play with.

15 chapters · in browser · self-contained

  1. I Geometry as the Language of Physics Two pillars built on geometry, where they break, and the bold step from worldlines to worldsheets.
  2. II Manifolds, Metrics, and the Action Principle Smooth manifolds, Lorentzian metrics, geodesics. The action principle as a geometric statement.
  3. III The Relativistic Point Particle A worldline as a curve. Reparametrization invariance. The simplest geometric action.
  4. IV From Worldline to Worldsheet Nambu–Goto and Polyakov. The string as a 2-surface swept through spacetime.
  5. V Symmetries of the Worldsheet Diffeomorphism invariance, Weyl rescaling, and the slide into conformal gauge.
  6. VI Solving the Classical String Modes, boundary conditions, and the geometric origin of D-branes.
  7. VII Quantization and the Conformal Algebra Oscillators become operators. The Virasoro algebra and its central charge.
  8. VIII The Critical Dimension Why bosonic strings demand 26 dimensions — and the regularization that puts it there.
  9. IX Strings on Curved Backgrounds The sigma model. Beta functions vanish → Einstein's equations re-emerge.
  10. X Superstrings and Fermions Worldsheet supersymmetry, the GSO projection, and the drop to ten dimensions.
  11. XI Compactification and Calabi–Yau Manifolds Hiding six dimensions in a Ricci-flat Kähler manifold of SU(3) holonomy.
  12. XII Dualities as Geometry T-duality, mirror symmetry, S-duality — and what they say about spacetime itself.
  13. XIII D-branes Submanifolds where open strings end, and the gauge theories that live on them.
  14. XIV Black Holes and Strominger–Vafa Counting brane microstates and recovering the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy exactly.
  15. XV Holography: AdS/CFT Maldacena's duality: gravity in a hyperbolic bulk equals gauge theory on its boundary.

Conventions used throughout: natural units $\hbar = c = 1$; mostly-plus signature $(-,+,+,\dots)$; target-space indices $\mu,\nu$; worldsheet indices $a,b$; worldsheet metric $h_{ab}$ (Polchinski writes $\gamma_{ab}$); string tension $T = 1/(2\pi\alpha')$; string length $\ell_s = \sqrt{\alpha'}$. Equation references follow Polchinski's String Theory (1998) unless noted.
